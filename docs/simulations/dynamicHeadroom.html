Analysis: What Does This Simulation Prove?

What this proves: The "headroom" is a safety buffer below your target limit - it gives your ESS (Energy Storage System) time to react before you exceed the limit. With dynamic headroom, this buffer adjusts based on battery SOC: when SOC is low (<20%), headroom is high (1.0 kW) because the battery can't help much. When SOC is high (>80%), headroom is low (0.2 kW) because the battery can quickly discharge if needed. This maximizes your usable power while maintaining safety. Watch the SOC chart and see how limits change as the battery charges overnight and discharges during peak hours.
